Roadmap

  1. Prepare soil
    • Remove weeds and debris from the field
    • Test soil pH and fertility
    • Add organic matter like compost or manure
    • Plow and till the soil to break it up and aerate it
    • Level the field for uniform irrigation.
  2. Plant Seeds
    • Select high-quality seeds suitable for the climate and soil
    • Treat seeds with fungicides or insecticides if necessary
    • Sow seeds at the right depth and spacing
    • Use proper planting techniques (direct seeding or transplanting)
    • Protect seeds from pests and birds
  3. Water Plants
    • Set up an efficient irrigation system (drip, sprinkler, or furrow)
    • Water plants regularly according to their needs
    • Monitor soil moisture levels
    • Adjust watering based on weather conditions.
    • Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot
  4. Fertilize Crops
    • Apply the right type and amount of fertilizer based on soil tests
    • Use a balanced fertilizer containing nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium
    • Schedule fertilization at critical growth stages
    • Consider using organic fertilizers or compost
    • Ensure even distribution of fertilizer across the field
  5. Harvest Crops
    • Monitor crop maturity and readiness for harvest
    • Use appropriate harvesting tools and machinery
    • Harvest crops during the cooler part of the day to reduce stress
    • Handle crops carefully to avoid damage
    • Store harvested crops in a cool, dry place to maintain quality.
